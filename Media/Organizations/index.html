Social Networking Media

The majority of organizations today aiding the effort against mass media’s negative effects are anti-cyber bullying groups. This includes groups such as the Cyberbullying Research Center1, StopBullying.gov2, and the American Humane Association.3 These groups offer education programs in various school districts and online resources educate parents and adolescents about the importance of maintaining anonymity online. Furthermore, these programs offer councilors, phone hotlines, and other advice for victims dealing with cyber bullying.  

The Durkheim Project is software that predicts whether an individual is suicidal based on language analysis algorithms that can be applied to text from social media websites. The algorithms are based on research of phrases, thought patterns, and specific beliefs that suicidal victims are more likely to say. The creators hope that this software will enable and train communities to recognize signs of those at risk of depression and suicide.

 

Video Games

Jack Thompson is an activist and former attorney well known for his work against sex and violence in video games. Thompson has analyzed and argued that games such as Counter-Strike and Grand Theft Auto are in essence murder simulations and “public safety hazards.”5 In addition to filing lawsuits against the companies responsible for publishing the games, Thompson has criticized numerous stores for selling mature (17+) rated games for people younger than the intended audience. Thompson calls video games “mental masturbation.”4 Thompson was active during the time when video game violence was a highly controversial topic from around 2002 to 2007. While his efforts helped raise awareness on violent video games, Thompson has garnered the enmity of many video gamers who believe many of his claims are far-fetched and not substantially backed by scientific research.

The Parents Television Council (PTC)6 pushes for laws that properly enforce the use of the age ratings set by the Entertainment Software Rating Board (ESRB). Like Jack Thompson, the PTC is displeased by the lack of control over adolescents purchasing games that are rated for higher ages. Many children are not required to show an ID showing proof age to purchase mature rated video games. This is similar to the availability of alcohol for purchase to minors.
