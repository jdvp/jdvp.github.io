Quick Facts:

·         97% of youth from 12-17 years of age play video games

·         1/3 of teenagers report a Mature or Adult Only rated game being in their top 3

·         Of the top 10 best-selling video games of 2014, 6 were violent and rated Mature

The only trend that is 100% true and proven is that adolescents do play violent video games and they play them a lot. The argument of whether or not violent video games cause adolescents to act violently has been going on for almost two decades. Truthfully, there is no direct or correct answer. It cannot be proven that playing violent video games has a causal relationship to adolescents performing violent behaviors. There are too many factors that play a major role in adolescent behavior to blame one factor such as the influence of a violent video game. Although there is no direct correlation between playing violent video games and performing violent behaviors, playing violent games can have serious negative effects on adolescent’s psycho-social development.

·         Long Hours/ Repetition = Subconscious Learning

·         Rewards for violence (Fantasy) vs Punishment for violence (Reality)

·         Paranoia

·         Desensitization

·         Pro-social vs Anti-social
